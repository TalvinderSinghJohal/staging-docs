## Overview

AltrumAI provides robust user management tools to help Admins control access and responsibilities across the organisation.
> **Note:**  
> The **Administration screen** is only accessible and visible to users with **Admin** privileges. All the steps outlined below are intended for **Admin users only**.

## Accessing User Management

1. Navigate to the **User Management** tab from the Navigation Panel
2. You will see a list of all current users and their roles

<img height="200" src="/images/acess-user-management.png" />

## Adding a New User (Admin Only)

1. Click on the **User Management** tab
2. Click the **Invite** button
3. Enter the user's email address
4. Select the appropriate **Role** (Admin or User)
5. Click **Invite** to send the invitation

![Placeholder for invite new user screenshot]

## Editing a User Role

1. From the **User Management** screen, locate the user.
2. Click on the **ellipse icon** in the **Actions** column for the user whose role you want to edit.
3. This opens a pop-up where you can edit the user's role.
4. Select the new role (Admin/User).
5. Click **Save** to apply the changes.

<img height="200" src="/images/edit-role.png" />

## Editing Account Details

1. In the top navigation bar, click on the **user icon** (shows your initials).
2. From the dropdown options, click on **Account** to open a pop-up with your user details.
3. Directly update the necessary user information in the editable fields.  
   > **Note:** Fields such as **Email**, **Role**, and **Organisation** are **not editable**.
4. Click **Save** to confirm and apply your changes.

![Placeholder for editing account details screenshot]

## Disabling a User

1. From the **User Management** screen, locate the user you want to disable.
2. Click on the **ellipse icon** in the **Actions** column for that user.
3. From the dropdown menu, select **Disable User**.
4. A confirmation pop-up will appear asking if you wish to disable the user.
5. Click **Disable** to confirm and complete the action.

![Placeholder for editing account details screenshot]

## User Roles Explained

- **Admin**: Full access to platform settings and user management
- **User**: Limited access, typically assigned to project-specific tasks

## Best Practices

- Regularly review user roles and statuses for compliance
- Only assign Admin roles to users who require elevated privileges
